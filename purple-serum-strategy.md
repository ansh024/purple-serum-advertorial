# Perfora Purple Magic Whitening Serum Advertorial Strategy

## Source Notes

- Product reviewed: [Perfora Purple Serum product page](https://perforacare.com/products/purple-magic-whitening-serum)
- Structure inspiration reviewed: [Fang enamel advertorial](https://fangoral.com/pages/enamel-v1)
- Positioning guardrail: present Purple Serum as an instant, temporary cosmetic colour-correction solution that can visibly neutralise yellow tones fast, while also referencing regular-use ingredients only where supported by the product page.

## 1. Hero

**Section objective**  
Stop the scroll by naming the real problem and immediately reframing Purple Serum as a quick confidence fix before important moments.

**UX reasoning**  
Mobile users need instant clarity on what the product does, who it is for, and why it is different from normal whitening. The first screen should reduce skepticism, not increase it.

**Complete copy**  
Headline: Yellow-looking teeth right before something important?  
Subheadline: Purple Magic Whitening Serum helps neutralise yellow tones on the surface of teeth in about 60 seconds using colour-correction technology, so your smile looks brighter for the moment that matters.  
Support line: Think of it like a cosmetic tone corrector for teeth, peroxide-free, enamel-safe, and easy to add to your routine.  
Micro-proof: 6,000+ reviews on Perfora's product page.  
Benefit chips: Instant appearance boost | Peroxide free | Enamel safe | Apple peach mint flavour

**CTA copy**  
Primary CTA: Try Purple Serum  
Secondary CTA: See How It Works

**Visual layout description**  
Split hero with premium editorial text stack on the left and product pedestal with floating proof cards on the right. On mobile, product visual appears between the headline and CTAs.

**Image requirements**  
IMAGE REQUIRED: Packshot of Purple Magic Whitening Serum on a soft white and lilac pedestal.  
IMAGE REQUIRED: Secondary beauty shot showing serum beside toothbrush foam.

**GIF requirements**  
GIF REQUIRED: Purple-to-yellow colour wheel animation showing neutralisation.

**Mobile behaviour**  
Headline capped to 4 lines, benefit chips become horizontal pills, CTAs stacked thumb-friendly, product image placed high.

**Conversion rationale**  
Frames the serum as immediate help for a near-term anxiety, which is more compelling than a generic whitening promise.

## 3. Why Teeth Look Yellow

**Section objective**  
Educate without shaming, and explain that yellow-looking teeth can come from both stains and natural enamel translucency.

**UX reasoning**  
Education lowers resistance and makes the product feel like a smart fix rather than a gimmick.

**Complete copy**  
Headline: Why teeth can look yellow even when you brush every day  
Body: Teeth are not naturally paper-white. Enamel is slightly translucent, and the layer beneath it can look warmer or darker. Add everyday staining from coffee, tea, smoking, and food pigments, and teeth can start looking more yellow than you want, especially in bright light and photos.  
Explainer points:
- Surface stains cling to the outer layer
- Enamel is not perfectly opaque
- Lighting can exaggerate yellow tones
- Whitening is not always instant

**CTA copy**  
CTA: Understand The Fix

**Visual layout description**  
Two-column explainer with a tooth cross-section illustration and four educational mini-cards.

**Image requirements**  
IMAGE REQUIRED: Clean medical-style illustration of enamel and dentin layers.  
IMAGE REQUIRED: Diagram showing stain buildup on tooth surface.

**GIF requirements**  
GIF REQUIRED: Simple animation of yellow tone sitting on the tooth surface.

**Mobile behaviour**  
Illustration first, then four stacked cards with short copy blocks.

**Conversion rationale**  
Sets up why an appearance-focused solution makes sense even if the user brushes consistently.

- Is the brightening effect permanent?  
  The colour-correction effect is cosmetic and temporary, designed to improve how teeth look in the moment.

## Build Notes

- Use a white base, muted lilac accents, charcoal typography, and rounded premium cards.
- Keep the page educational first and sales-led second.
- Include a sticky mobile CTA anchored near the bottom of the viewport.
- Keep all image slots as explicit placeholders until the brand supplies approved creative.
- For Shopify conversion, the HTML sections can map cleanly into Liquid sections/blocks with configurable image pickers, review data, and CTA URLs.
